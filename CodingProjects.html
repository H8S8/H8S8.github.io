Penetration of Neutrons Through Shielding

May 2022

A program to calculate the total impedance of an analogue circuit was created. From an abstract base class for circuit components, classes for a resistor, capacitor, and an inductor were derived. After constructing a library of components, the user is then able to build a circuit using components from the library by making use of polymorphism. Smart pointers are utilised throughout the code to create vectors of base class pointers. Two types of visual output of the circuit are available to the user. Once the frequency of the circuit is set by the user, the impedance and phase-shift of the whole circuit and each individual component is returned.

Penetration of Neutrons Through Shielding

March 2021

In this project Monte Carlo simulations were used to model the behaviour of thermal neutrons as they penetrate various shielding materials. Considering the only interactions of the neutrons with the shielding material atoms to be either absorption or scattering, the fraction of neutrons absorbed, reflected, or transmitted through varying thicknesses of water, lead, or graphite was calculated, along with the corresponding characteristic attenuation lengths. The effect of multiple slabs of different materials on the transmission of the neutrons was also investigated using the woodcock method.

The Damped Harmonic Oscillator

March 2021

We have studied the effect of different damping constants using Verlet's method with the chosen timestep ‚Ñé=0.1ùë† , which approximated the analytical solution very well in all of the cases. The effect of a sudden force on free, undamped oscillations was then investigated. It was found that a 'push' at the equilibrium position would either increase or decrease the amplitude of oscillation. A push at the amplitude of oscillation would increase the amplitude of oscillation and introduce a phase shift. When driven with a sinusoidal forcing function, the amplitude of oscillation increases as the driving frequency gets closer to the natural frequency of the oscillator. Verlet's method approximated the resonance and phase difference curves well, the plots of sinusoidally forced oscillation indicated the same results that were found from these curves.
